# Excl-Serious-RoB sensitivity — decision (2026-07, new ROBINS-I ratings)

Under the signalling-question ROBINS-I ratings, of the 16 assessable poolable observational
studies: 13 Serious, 3 Moderate, 0 Low. The 3 Moderate studies are:
- 30297350 (suicide attempt/self-harm)
- 39226030 (completed suicide)
- 40010803 (suicidality (composite))

Each Moderate study sits in a DIFFERENT outcome pool, so an "exclude Serious/Critical RoB"
sensitivity pool would leave <=1 study per outcome — no outcome retains k>=2. The sensitivity
analysis is therefore not estimable and is NOT reported as a pooled row (this is why
pooled_estimates.csv has no excl-Serious-RoB stratum). The finding to report is qualitative:
the observational evidence base is almost entirely Serious RoB (confounding by indication,
D1), so no subset of low-RoB studies exists to pool. This is itself a GRADE-relevant fact and
is reflected in every observational stratum being rated Very low certainty.
