# Falsification, residual uncertainty, and what would flip each conclusion

A four-stream convergence is exactly the pattern a hostile reviewer suspects of
motivated analysis. This section states precisely what the evidence **cannot**
rule out and what specific finding would overturn each conclusion. The
conclusions are held only as strongly as these bounds allow.

## Conclusion 1 — "No causal neuropsychiatric harm from GLP-1 RAs (class level)"

**What supports it:** RCT pooled 0.84 (0.54–1.32); observational 0.71–0.94;
registry AE RR 0.86 (0.61–1.22); FAERS null under the comparator design before
July 2023. Three independent designed-evidence channels, one spontaneous-report
channel, all null-to-protective.

**What it does NOT establish, and cannot with this corpus:**
1. **Molecule-specific harm below pooling power.** The class estimate is robust,
   but a single molecule could carry a small effect masked by pooling. The
   semaglutide FAERS signal is explained by notoriety (Step 2), *but* the RCT
   corpus is underpowered to exclude, say, a molecule-specific RR of 1.3 on
   completed suicide. **Would flip it:** a large, adequately powered,
   single-molecule RCT or cohort (≥10⁵ person-years) showing a CI excluding 1.0
   for that molecule.
2. **Baseline-psychiatric-history effect modification.** Individuals with prior
   depression/suicidality may respond differently. This is **not estimable
   here** — no trial subgroup data by psychiatric history, and the FAERS
   co-medication proxy is confounded. **Would flip it:** a stratified analysis
   (RCT subgroup or matched cohort) showing an interaction with prior
   psychiatric history, CI excluding the null.
3. **Adolescents / young adults.** The corpus is adult, mixed-population. The
   FDA's own concern history centred partly on younger patients. Nothing here
   speaks to under-18s or a distinct young-adult effect. **Would flip it:** a
   paediatric/adolescent trial or cohort with a positive, precise estimate.
4. **Longer latency than trial follow-up.** Most RCTs follow 1–2 years; a
   delayed effect is not excluded. **Would flip it:** a long-horizon cohort
   (≥5 yr) with a rising hazard.

## Conclusion 2 — "The spontaneous-report harm signal is notoriety bias + confounding by indication"

**What supports it:** within-comparator pre-July-2023 null (0.85) vs post (2.98);
neutral headache control flat across the boundary; signal tracks the brands the
July-2023 probe named, not report volume or receptor pharmacology (tirzepatide
protective); calibrated tool.

**What it does NOT establish:**
1. **That there is zero true signal buried in the notoriety surge.** Notoriety
   inflation and a small true effect are not mutually exclusive; we show the
   *observed* signal is fully accountable by notoriety timing, not that a true
   effect is impossible. **Would flip it:** a pre-2023, adequately powered
   disproportionality signal that survives the comparator design — which the
   data do not show, but a different database (e.g. WHO VigiBase with more
   pre-2023 volume) could in principle reveal.
2. **The adjudication percentages are soft.** The "11% plausibly
   drug-attributed" is model-dependent (κ ≈ 0.4–0.5; 14% vs 22% between models).
   The notoriety conclusion does **not** rest on it — it rests on the time-split
   — but the case-mix percentages themselves should not be over-read.

## Conclusion 3 — "Cross-addiction benefit direction (Stream D, EXPLORATORY)"

**What supports it (hypothesis-generating only):** of 47 direction-eligible studies
(52 extracted − 5 excluded), 28 benefit / 13 mixed / 6 no-effect / 0 harm — a
vote-count, which is vulnerable to study size, selective reporting, and publication
bias and is **not** an inferential argument; exploratory pooled estimates nicotine
RCT+TTE 0.68, alcohol 0.69; and an inverse FAERS footprint (inverse disproportionality,
not "benefit"). "0 harm" means no study reported a pro-addiction direction, not
evidence of safety.

**What it does NOT establish:** certainty is **Very low** (observational,
heterogeneous, I² up to 88%). **Would flip it:** a large dedicated
addiction-endpoint RCT (several are ongoing) returning null. The benefit direction is
held at "promising but very uncertain / exploratory," and the benefit–harm "symmetry"
is a narrative framing, not a statistical property — the primary safety question
(Stream A/B) is kept separate from this exploratory question.

## Conclusion 4 — "Mechanism favours benefit / neither"

**What it does NOT establish:** mechanism is plausibility, not effect size, and
Stream C is the thinnest stream. A mechanistic account is compatible with the
null; it does not prove it. **Would flip the plausibility read:** a demonstrated
GLP-1R-dependent pro-suicidal pathway in mesolimbic circuitry — none is
established.

## The single finding that would most damage the overall synthesis
A **pre-July-2023, comparator-controlled, adequately powered** analysis (any
database or design) showing a suicide/self-injury signal for any single molecule
with a CI excluding 1.0. That is the load-bearing null. Everything else is
corroboration around it. We looked for it in FAERS and did not find it; we cannot
exclude that it exists in a data source we did not analyse (VigiBase, full
Embase/CENTRAL, raw FAERS/MedWatch narratives via FOIA).

## Honest scope statement
This synthesis establishes, with **moderate-to-high confidence at the class
level**, the *absence of a large causal neuropsychiatric harm* and a *probable
low-certainty cross-addiction benefit*. It does **not** establish safety for
every molecule, every subgroup, every age band, or every latency. Those are the
legitimate residual, and they are where the next designed study should aim.
